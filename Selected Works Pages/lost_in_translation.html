Artist Statement:

"Lost in Translation" is a physical art installation where human participants will enter a "phonebooth" and try to help a "bot", lost and in trouble on phone. This is a play on the frustrating human experience of trying to receive help from corporate AI phone systems, where our exhibition now flips the roles between robot and human. We hope to relay the confusion that miscounnication can bring, particularly in the space of technology, but also relating to the experiences of immigrants from foreign countries. By utilizing speech recognition, language translation, and the words from previous participants, each experience is unique and different from the last.

We will delved into ciruitry, AI audio distortion, installation art, and storytelling.

My Contributions:

In this project, I had contributions in many different areas. I mainly worked on rewiring the phone to work in conjunction with the computer. Additionally, in the script, I worked on creating the functions that used AI to turn audio into text and another function that translated and distorted the language. I worked a lot on assembling the physical installation, making purchasing choices, and making design choices. Furthermore, I had some contributions to creating the map in the physical installation. Our team worked on the storyline together throughout the development process.

Learned soldering techniques to rewire a Western Electric rotary phone. The microphone input is redirected into a computer, processed through a Python script, and sent back out the phone's speaker.

Process of creating the physical installation. The inside walls incorporate a map. The user has to guide the bot on the phone through the map to get home. The responses from the bot are dependent on the user's audio.

Progression of memories of past users being written on the wall. This segment of the peice was supposed connect the different users of the experience. The bot shared previous memories as its own and asked the next user to write it on the wall. This was to show the deterioration of language as it is translated and processed.
